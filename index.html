Keyboard-First,
Local Markdown
Note-Taking

QuickDown is a free, fast and customizable notepad that sits on top of local plain text Markdown files

It allows you to capture your ideas quickly NOW while staying focused,
then flesh out your ideas LATER with your preferred Markdown editor
like Obsidian, LogSeq, OneNote, VS Code, Zettlr, Vim (or any other plain text editor, really.)

{% include cta.html %}

"Keyboard-First"

Write, edit and save your ideas to Markdown files without ever leaving your keyboard. Just like a real notepad, QuickDown is built from the ground up to feel like pen-and-paper.

To capture a new idea, you have one global hotkey that works anywhere and anytime. Your cursor is immediately focused to go from idea to typing your first letter in 0.42ms or less.

"Local Markdown"

Your notes are saved locally as plain text Markdown files. As such, they're fully backup-able and future-proof. You can store them in any folder you want including iCloud, Google drive, DropBox and OneDrive.

Save time with custom markdown templates for new notes. Pre-fill your note with live-variables like today's date, time, an auto-incrementing ID. And control exactly where you want your cursor to be placed when you start writing.

"Note-Taking"

No need to switch to a behemoth markdown editor just to take a quick note. To keep you focused and avoid distraction, QuickDown is intentionally bare-bones and only appears when you really need it.

It is a lightweight companion to your preferred knowledge base like Obsidian, LogSeq, VS Code, iA Writer, Zettlr, and TiddlyWiki (or any other plain-text based writing process, really.)

Alexis here, great to meet you!

I'm an avid Obsidian user and constant pen-and-paper note-taker.

Taking a quick note with pen and paper is fast and easy, right?

That's when I had to ask myself:

WHY ON EARTH IS TAKING A SIMPLE DIGITAL NOTE STILL SO UTTERLY AWKWARD AND DISTRACTING?!

–––

Even for jotting down a single sentence you'd have to...

1. Launch Obsidian (or another full-blown Markdown editor)

2. Switch my current focus to Obsidian

3. Ignore a thousand tempting rabbit-holes (My notes)

4. Create a new note

5. Leave my keyboard

6. Mouse-click to focus the correct pane

7. Switch back to my keyboard

8. Write a few lines

9. Switch back to what I was working on before the idea appeared

This is 2022 and that's an INSANE amount of steps, you know?!

–––

For me, Obsidian is perfect for "LATER".

"LATER", when I flesh out notes, connect ideas, write a first draft, edit.

But until then, all I need is to capture my idea NOW. And IN ONE GO.

And none of the existing native note-taking apps had what I needed.

–––

So I made my own app that lets me take a note without losing focus:

1. A single global hotkey ⌘-⌥-N pops up a new note and focuses my cursor

2. A simple ⌘-S saves the markdown note to my Obsidian vault

3. A simple Esc dismisses the popover

To type even less boilerplate, I added custom templates and live-variables.

–––

QuickDown is the least-frills way to jot down a markdown note.

And that's it.

Nothing more, nothing less.

 

And I hope you enjoy it as much as I do.

Cheers and with best wishes!

 

ALEXIS
