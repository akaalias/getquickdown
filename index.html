Note-Taking

For people who want to quickly capture ideas NOW and flesh them out LATER with their preferred knowledge base such as Obsidian, LogSeq, VS Code, iA Writer, Zettlr, TiddlyWiki (or any other plain-text based writing process, really.)

QuickDown is a free, blazing-fast, no-frills, tiny and customizable notepad that sits on top of local plain text Markdown files

{% include cta.html %}

"Keyboard-First"

Write, edit and save your ideas to Markdown files without ever leaving your keyboard. Just like a real notepad, QuickDown is built from the ground up to feel like pen-and-paper.

To capture a new idea, you have one global hotkey that works anywhere and anytime. Your cursor is immediately focused to go from idea to typing your first letter in 0.42ms or less.

"Local Markdown"

Your notes are saved locally as plain text Markdown files. As such, they're fully backup-able and future-proof, you can store them in any folder you want including iCloud, Google drive, DropBox and OneDrive.

Save time with custom markdown templates for new notes. Start with your preferred document structure, pre-fill your template with today's date, time and clipboard-content and control exactly where you want your cursor to be placed when you start writing.

"Note-Taking"

No need to switch to a behemoth markdown editor just to take a quick note. To keep you focused and avoid distraction, QuickDown is intentionally bare-bones, always-in-the-background yet only visible when you need it.

It is a lightweight companion to your preferred knowledge base like Obsidian, LogSeq, VS Code, iA Writer, Zettlr, and TiddlyWiki (or any other plain-text based writing process, really.)

As an avid Obsidian user and constant pen-paper note-taker, I often want to take a quick markdown note while working BUT...

– without having to keep Obsidian running in the background

– without having to leave my keyboard

Only LATER do I want to connect and remix them in Obsidian only at specific moments.

None of the existing note-taking menu bar apps had what I needed. So I made my own that fits my needs perfectly.

1. A single global hotkey ⌘-⌥-N creates a new note

2. A simple ⌘-S saves the markdown note into my Obsidian vault

3. A simple Esc dismisses the popover

That's how QuickDown came about: Just a way to jot down a markdown note.

Nothing more, nothing less.

I hope QuickDown will help you as much as it has helped me!
