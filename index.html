"Keyboard-First"

Write, edit and save your ideas to Markdown files without ever leaving your keyboard. Just like a real notepad, QuickDown is built from the ground up to feel like pen-and-paper.

To capture a new idea, you have one global hotkey that works anywhere and anytime. Your cursor is immediately focused to go from idea to typing your first letter in 0.42ms or less.

"Local Markdown"

Your notes are saved locally as plain text Markdown files. As such, they're fully backup-able and future-proof, you can store them in any folder you want including iCloud, Google drive, DropBox and OneDrive.

Save time with custom markdown templates for new notes. Start with your preferred document structure, pre-fill your template with today's date, time and clipboard-content and control exactly where you want your cursor to be placed when you start writing.

"Note-Taking"

No need to switch to a behemoth markdown editor just to take a quick note. To keep you focused and avoid distraction, QuickDown is intentionally bare-bones, always-in-the-background and only visible when you need it.

It is a lightweight companion to your preferred knowledge base like Obsidian, LogSeq, VS Code, iA Writer, Zettlr, and TiddlyWiki (or any other plain-text based writing process, really.)

Dear friend,

Alexis here! Hi.

I'm an avid Obsidian user and constant pen-and-paper note-taker.

Using pen and paper is fast, easy and effortless.

So recently I had to ask myself:

Why on Earth is taking digital notes STILL SO AWKWARD AND DISTRACTING?

–––

Even for jotting down a quick two-liner I have to...

1. Launch Obsidian (or another full-blown Markdown editor)

2. Switch my current focus to Obsidian

3. Ignore the rabbit-holes (notes, links, plugins) I created there for myself

4. Create a new note

5. Leave my keyboard

6. Mouse-click to focus the correct pane

7. Switch back to my keyboard

8. Write a few lines

8. Switch back to what I was working on before the idea appeared

–––

You know, knowledge bases and editors are perfect for "LATER".

Later: When I want to connect ideas, draft an outline, flesh out the story, edit.

But until then, all I need is to capture my idea NOW and IN ONE GO.

–––

None of the existing native note-taking apps had what I needed.

So I made my own that fits my personal needs:

1. A single global hotkey ⌘-⌥-N creates a new note

2. A simple ⌘-S saves the markdown note to my Obsidian vault

3. A simple Esc dismisses the popover

PLUS: Bootstrap notes with custom markdown templates

PLUS: Add context with live-variables like %DATE%, %TIME% and %ID%

 

QuickDown is the least-frills way to jot down a markdown note.

And that's it. Nothing more, nothing less.

 

Cheers and with best wishes!
